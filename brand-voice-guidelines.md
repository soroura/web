# Ahmed Sorour — Personal Brand Voice Guidelines

---

## 1. Brand Essence

**One-line identity:** Public health and disaster medicine specialist, focused on emergency care, who builds the tools the field is missing.

**Core narrative:** Ahmed Sorour works at the intersection of public health, disaster medicine, emergency care, and applied technology. His career arc — from offshore rigs and emergency departments to WHO policy rooms and AI-augmented platforms — is unified by a single thread: closing the gap between what health systems need and what practitioners actually have.

**What makes this voice distinct:** Ahmed doesn't write as an academic, a technologist, or a bureaucrat. He writes as a practitioner who builds. Every idea is grounded in field experience. Every tool traces back to a problem he watched happen. The voice carries the authority of someone who's been in the room where things go wrong — and then went and built something about it.

## 5. Signature Phrases and Patterns

**Recurring motifs:**
- "The gap between..." (policy and practice, tools and needs, what exists and what's needed)
- "So I built..." (transition from observation to action)
- "What actually happens is..." (field reality vs. theory)
- "The tools haven't caught up with the people"

**Structural patterns:**
- Open with a concrete observation, not an abstract claim
- Use short sentences for emphasis after longer explanatory ones
- Let credentials speak through work described, not titles listed
- End sections with forward momentum — what's next, not what was

**Avoid:**
- "Passionate about" / "Dedicated to" / "Driven by"
- Buzzword stacking: "leveraging synergies to drive impact"
- Third-person self-reference on a personal site
- Superlatives without substance ("world-class", "cutting-edge", "revolutionary")
- Listing job titles as identity — the work speaks

## 6. Audience

**Primary:** Health professionals, WHO/MoH colleagues, emergency care practitioners, simulation exercise community, potential collaborators

**Secondary:** Data science and health tech communities, academic peers, conference organisers, global health network

**Tertiary:** Anyone curious about the intersection of public health, disaster medicine, emergency care systems, and applied technology

**What they're looking for:** Someone who understands both the field reality and the technical possibilities. Not a pitch — a perspective.

---

## 7. Content Principles

1. **Show the work.** Describe what was built, what it does, how it works. Let that carry the argument.
2. **Name the problem first.** Every project page, every blog post starts with the problem that prompted it.
3. **Be specific.** "22 EMRO countries" not "multiple countries." "105 API endpoints" not "a comprehensive API."
4. **Connect the dots.** The power of Ahmed's profile is the unusual combination — public health + disaster medicine + emergency care focus + data science + building. Make those connections visible.
5. **Write for the practitioner.** Someone who's run a drill, managed a ward, sat in a coordination meeting. They should recognise their own experience in the writing.
6. **Respect the reader's time.** Short pages. Clear structure. No scroll for the sake of scroll.

## 8. Visual and Formatting Guidelines

**Design concept:** "Field Notes" — a practitioner's notebook aesthetic with a Slate & Ember colour palette. Cool editorial precision with a single warm accent that draws the eye. The site reads like something a builder carries with them: controlled, grounded, decisive. Not a startup landing page, not an academic CV.

**Colour palette:** Cool slate base with ember red accent. Primary accent: #dc2626 (red-600). Light: #fef2f2 (rose-50). Hover: #b91c1c (red-700). Warm accent: #ef4444 (red-500). Backgrounds: slate-50 (#f8fafc) and slate-100 (#f1f5f9). Paper: white (#ffffff). Deep: #0f172a (slate-900). Mid: #334155 (slate-700). Borders: slate-200 (#e2e8f0). Dark mode: slate-900 (#0f172a) base, slate-800 (#1e293b) alt, ember brightens to #ef4444. Auto dark mode via `prefers-color-scheme`.

**Typography:** DM Serif Display (Google Fonts) for headlines — editorial weight without being academic. Inter for body text. Serif at regular weight for a confident, readable display. Body at 0.95rem with 1.85 line-height. Hero provocation at 2.75rem.

**Layout:** 1080px max for homepage sections, 720px narrow column for prose pages (about, field notes, contact). "Notes grid" with 1px gap borders for topic cards. Generous whitespace. Content-first hierarchy.

**Navigation:** Flat, minimal — Builds, Field Notes, About, "Say Hello" (amber-bordered CTA). Sticky with subtle scroll border. Serif site name. No dropdowns, no hamburger on desktop. Mobile: slide-down menu.

**Decorative elements:** Ember field-marker (40px horizontal rule) as section dividers. Ember-red left-border on blockquotes with rose-50 background. Hover reveals: ember bar slides in on card hover. Controlled intensity — one warm element against cool slate.

**Images:** Used sparingly and with purpose. No stock photography. If there's a photo, it shows real work in real settings.

**Social sharing:** OG tags on every page. Theme-color meta set to #334155 (slate-700) for mobile browser chrome.

## 9. Quick Reference Card

| Element | Guideline |
|---------|----------|
| **First person** | Yes — "I", not "Ahmed Sorour is..." |
| **Sentence length** | Mix. Short for impact. Longer for explanation. |
| **Paragraphs** | Short. 2-4 sentences. |
| **Headers** | Descriptive, not clever. Tell the reader what's coming. |
| **Links** | Inline and purposeful. No "click here." |
| **Jargon** | Use field-specific terms (EXCON, TTX, IHR) but provide context on first use |
| **Humour** | Dry, observational, rare. Never forced. |
| **Call to action** | Soft. "Get in touch" not "Contact me NOW" |
