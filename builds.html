SimReady

In Development · 11 Releases Shipped

An AI-augmented simulation exercise platform that covers the full exercise lifecycle: Plan, Design, Conduct, Evaluate. SimReady exists because simulation exercises — the single most effective tool for testing emergency preparedness — are still designed on paper, facilitated with walkie-talkies, and evaluated from memory.

The platform includes an 8-step AI Scenario Generator covering 12 exercise classes across 22 EMRO countries, a live Exercise Runner with a Unified Inject Queue, six realism modules (chat, PTT radio, SitRep dashboards, news/social media, patient tracking, document exchange), and an evaluation engine that produces AI-generated After-Action Reviews with cross-exercise analytics.

Built offline-first with CRDT sync, multi-language support (Arabic RTL, English, French, Spanish), and an MCP server with 28 tools for conversational exercise management through AI assistants.

National Assessment Platform

In Development

A digital framework consolidating WHO-aligned assessment tools and checklists into a single platform for national-level health facility evaluation and quality improvement. Designed for hospital teams, quality officers, and national programme coordinators who currently work across disconnected spreadsheets and paper forms.

Covers patient safety (PSFHI), disaster preparedness, mass casualty management, and emergency care readiness — bringing multiple assessment domains into one coherent system that supports gap identification, improvement tracking, and evidence-based reporting.

MSc Dissertation: TraumaApp ED Data Analysis

In Progress · University of Edinburgh

A work-based dissertation analysing real-life data from the TraumaApp pilot at a Scottish hospital. The TraumaApp captures timed steps of the emergency department journey with a granularity that traditional registries cannot provide — making it possible to examine the factors that affect ED length of stay and time to definitive care.
